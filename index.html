About Me

I am a soil biodiversity researcher with over a decade of experience working in Kenya. My research interests include arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi (AMF), bacteria and fungi, nematodes, and soil macrofauna. I am passionate about understanding the role of soil biodiversity in ecosystem function and sustainable agriculture. I hold a Master of Science degree in Soil Science from the University of Bangor, Wales.

My research focused on a long-term agroforestry trial with pure and mixed stands, and the effects of this on AMF diversity and abundance. I am currently a research scientist at CIFOR-ICRAF. In my current role, I am engaged on various projects majoring in rangelands restorations, agroecology, and soil health. I am a skilled researcher and communicator. I am also a highly motivated and results-oriented individual.

My goal is to make significant contributions to the field of soil biodiversity research through collaborations, outreach, and education. I believe that by working together, we can make a real difference in protecting this vital resource for future generations.
